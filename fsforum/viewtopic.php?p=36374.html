Re: Problem: Blackout function of Freestyler works not corre

  • Quote

Post by alembala » September 15th, 2014, 11:42 am

I have found a workaround.

First I found an note of "Spirit":

blackout depant on the Channels you use..
and FS looks at it in ONE order..

1. IF Fixture has Shutter, then Shutter CLOSED is used for Blackout.
2. IF Fixture Doesnt have Shutter, BUT intensitie channel, Then Intensitie MIN is used for Blackout
3. IF fixture doesnt have Shutter or Intensitie, BUT RGB channels, then RGB "0" is used for Blackout..

based to this Information I was able to reconfigure the fixture with fixture creator. But if Macro channel (6) or Color channel (4) is in use the blackout will not work!
That is really a tricky thing.

You can force the blackout to Color channel (4) and macro channel (6) if you define them in fixture creater as Amber channel and a White channel.
The blackout works then to 5 of 6 channels. But ATTENTION! if you use the master intensity in freestyler it will change the values of Amber and White channel too. This means the master itensity does not work properly if channel 4 and 6 are used!

Finally you have to decide if you keep in mind that Blackout does not work if the two channels of fixtures are used or you have a always working blackout but issues with master intensity if channel 4 and 6 are used. Normally the intensity master has no effect if macro or Color channel is used here (means lights not increased).

So my result is, it could be helpful if freestyler has a fourth option like do blackout over a macro.
Is this something I should post as suggestion for a new feature of freestyler?
